This online Microphone Test helps you verify if your microphone is working correctly. You can see real-time audio visualization through waveforms and spectrograms, record and play back audio, and test different microphone inputs if you have multiple devices connected.

Common Microphone Issues & Solutions:

  • No Sound Detected:
    • Permission Issues: Make sure you've granted browser permission to access your microphone.
    • Wrong Input Device: Check if the correct microphone is selected in the dropdown menu.
    • System Settings: Verify the microphone isn't muted in your system's sound settings.
    • Driver Problems: Try updating or reinstalling your audio drivers.
  • Low Volume or Quiet Recording:
    • Input Level: Adjust the microphone's input level in your system settings.
    • Physical Position: Ensure the microphone is properly positioned and not obstructed.
    • Boost Settings: Check if microphone boost is enabled in your system's advanced audio settings.
  • Static or Background Noise:
    • Interference: Keep microphone cables away from power cables and electronic devices.
    • USB Port Issues: Try a different USB port if using a USB microphone.
    • Audio Enhancement: Disable any audio enhancement features to test if they are causing interference.
  • Microphone Not Recognized:
    • Connection: Try unplugging and reconnecting your microphone.
    • Device Compatibility: Ensure your microphone is compatible with your system.
    • Operating System: Check for any pending system updates that might affect audio devices.
Use the waveform and spectrogram displays to visually diagnose issues - a properly working microphone should show clear response to sound with minimal background noise.

Using the Record and Playback Feature:
  • Recording:
    • Click the record button to start capturing audio
    • Speak normally at your typical distance from the microphone
    • Try different volumes and sounds to test the full range
    • Click stop when finished recording
  • Playback Analysis:
    • Play back the recording to hear exactly how your microphone captures sound
    • Listen for any background noise, static, or distortion
    • Check if your voice sounds clear and at an appropriate volume
    • Compare the playback using different audio outputs (speakers/headphones) to ensure the issue is with the microphone
